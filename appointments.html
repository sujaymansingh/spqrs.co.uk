Guidelines

  • For morning appointments please call at 8am, for evening appointments please call at 3pm
  • Urgent cases are seen on the day.
  • If your condition is non-urgent, you may have to wait longer.
  • Pre-booked appointments are booked up to a week in advance and can be booked anytime during the week
  • Tell us if you are in a telephone box. Say immediately if your call is an emergency. We will then try to ring you back.
  • Let us know if more than one person in the family needs to be seen. We can give you a longer appointment if necessary.
  • Tell us if you want someone to accompany you during an examination or a private room to discuss any matters. Remember that the results of tests can only be given to the patient.
  • You can have a telephone consultation with a doctor. He or she will ring you on the telephone number you have given. If you are only available on a mobile phone, you will be expected to call the practice at an agreed time.

You can help us by

  • Being on time for your appointment
  • Letting us know if you need to cancel
  • Calling for a home visit or urgent appointment before 10am
  • Ringing for the results of tests after 11 am.

Home Visits

Our doctors typically see three patients in the practice in the time it takes to do a single home visit.

For this reason, we ask our patients to come to the practice if at all possible.

However, we can visit you at home if your circumstances prevent you from attending our practices. Please ring before 10am to arrange a visit and let us know if your condition is urgent.
